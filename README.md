# Data Analytics Portfolio
**By Aalaa Jandali**
*Product Owner turned Data Analyst | SQL, Python & Operational Strategy*

Welcome to my data portfolio. I have 6+ years of experience as an Agile Product Owner, and I am currently pursuing a Master's in Data Analytics at Western Governors University. This portfolio will document both personal and academic projects I worked on during my data analytics learning journey.

## 📂 Projects

- **[Balaji Fast Food — Sales Analysis](balaji-fast-food-sales-analysis/)** — Python + Jupyter cleaning and EDA of 1,000 fast-food orders, a written business report, and an interactive [Tableau dashboard](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/aalaa.jandali/viz/Restaurant_Sales_Dashboard_v2/RestaurantSalesDashboard) answering what sells, when, how customers pay, and how the business trends.
- **[Medical Appointment No-Shows — SQL Analysis](medical-appointment-no-shows-sql/)** — SQL-first analysis of 110K medical appointments from Kaggle: loading, profiling, and cleaning data entirely in SQL, then using aggregations, CTEs, and window functions to find why 20% of patients miss their appointments. Finds that booking lead time and a patient's own no-show history are the strongest predictors — and untangles an SMS-reminder "paradox" that reverses once the confounder is controlled for. Includes a written [findings report](medical-appointment-no-shows-sql/report/findings.md) and a live [Tableau dashboard](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/aalaa.jandali/viz/Medical_No_Show_Dashboard/MedicalAppointmentNoShows).
- **[Hospital Readmission Prediction](hospital-readmission-prediction/)** — Predictive modeling on 101K diabetic inpatient encounters (UCI / Cerner Health Facts): SQL (DuckDB) cleaning and feature engineering, then **logistic regression** in Python to rank patients by 30-day readmission risk for targeted follow-up. Centers on the **accuracy trap** — a model that flags no one scores 91% accuracy — and reports odds ratios, AUC, and a gains curve instead. Contacting the top 20% of patients by predicted risk reaches 36% of eventual readmissions (1.8× better than random). Includes a written [findings report](hospital-readmission-prediction/report/findings.md) and a six-page Power BI dashboard for the results.
